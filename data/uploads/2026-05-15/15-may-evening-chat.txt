03:03:59	Pavani Ayinampudi:	What is Rosetta?
The Rosetta Stone was discovered in 1799. It carried the same text in three scripts, and it became the key to decoding an entire language that had been silent for centuries. Not because it was grand — but because it was honest and consistent.
This journal is your Rosetta. Sixty-five days of honest entries will become the key to understanding your own experience — what you learned, where you struggled, what shifted in you. Not for us. For you.
How to use it
One entry per day. Every day. No skipping.
Each day has a thinking routine — a short framework to help you reflect in a specific way. They rotate so the journal does not feel mechanical. Some will feel easy. Some will make you stop and actually think. That is the point.
One rule only
Write what is true. Not what sounds impressive, not what you think we want to read, not a polished summary. If you hated today, write that. If you are confused, write that. If something clicked for the first time, write that.
